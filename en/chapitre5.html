Chapter 5
The Challenges
The prisonner
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aphrodite finally had her prey. But she was still pregnant – and worse, from her son! Impossible in this circumstance to get rid of her this way. Hera, goddess of family, would never accept it. She locked Psyche in her cave and thought about what to do. She must give Psyche missions that could not fulfill. Then, Aphrodite could punish the princess. Even the goddess Hera could not prevent a goddess the right to punish a mortal who had disobeyed.
Ants
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aphrodite summoned Psyche: “you will only be able to save your life if from now to this evening you sort and organize all the seeds you see here by species.” After this, Aphrodite ran off to a wedding where she was invited. There were thousands of seeds and she knew she needed help. Thankfully, an ant was passing by. Psyche had her climb on her finger and begged her to come to her assistance. Touched, the small animal went to go get all her sisters who went to work, each carrying a seed on her back. When Aphrodite got back, at her great surprise and unhappiness, she noticed that all the seeds we organized and sorted.
The golden lambs
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
“Bring me back the wool of golden lambs”, was the second challenge. These lambs covered in golden wool were eating in a clearing. The mission seemed easy, so Psyche worried about a trap. Near the clearing ran a small stream. Psyche kneeled and pretended to clean herself in the stream and asked the stream for advice.
“These lambs are actually ferocious beasts that devour all people who approach them. But before dusk, they leave to go to the heart of the forest. Follow them at a distance and pick up the wool that they leave behind them on the limbs and branches of bushes and trees.” Psyche wisely followed their advice and once again, Aphrodite could only see her success in this task.
The river Styx
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
“Go fill this vial with the water from the spring of the river Styx”. This water before diving deep into hell, burst at the crest of a tall mountain. How could pregnant Psyche climb such an immense rocky cliff? Once again, she knew she needed help. She saw circling high in the sky a great eagle. She called him and told him her story. Like the reed and the ant, the bird had the heart to assist a princess so courageous. He took the vial and went off to fill it at the top of the mountain. For the third time, Aphrodite was forced to recognize the victory of Psyche.
Kingdom of the dead
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
“This will be your last mission: bring me back a bit of the beauty of Persephone. It is the kind of thing we do between goddesses.” Persephone was none other than the wife of Hades, the god of Hell! Psyche must then go to the heart of the kingdom of the dead and come back. No other challenge could be more frightening but the young princess armed herself with courage and descended into the abyss.
She passed the terrifying Cerberus and Charon, the ferryman, had her cross Styx on his boat. When she presented herself to the infernal couple, she thought her heart would stop beating but she was bold enough to throw herself at the feet of Persephone and ask for her pity.
To her great surprise, the wife of the king of the dead accepted her request and gave her a box. “Bring it directly to Aphrodite and most importantly do not open it: what it contains should not be seen by a mortal.” Without any issues Psyche went back the same way she came, passing by the terrifying monsters that usually don’t let anyone leave. Once in the open air, her curiosity became too great: she had within her hands the opportunity to find the secret of the queen of hell! She opened the box. A vapor escaped that made her fall asleep in a deep lethargic slumber.
Renuion
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aphrodite was triumphant. However, Eros, finally recovered from his wounds, woke up. Fooling his mother’s supervision, he went off to on a quest to find Psyche who he found asleep underneath a great leafy oak. To extract her from her sleep, he poked her with one of his arrows: do we not say that love’s pangs can wake up the dead? The princess opened her eyes and had a cry of surprise: she has finally found her lover.
The wedding of Eros and Psyche
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Eros brought Psyche before Zeus, the king of Gods. The vengeance of his mother has sufficiently lasted and the princess had gone through enough hardship. The goddess Hera interceded in her favor: the affair has lasted too long and caused too much chaos. She made Aphrodite understand that there comes a point where a mother should let her child decide his destiny and leave him choose the woman he wants.
Zeus gave his verdict: He gave Psyche Ambrosia so that could become immortal like the gods. All of Olympus then celebrated the wedding of the two lovers. Shortly after, the child of Eros and Psyche was bor. It was a daughter. She was called Hedone.
