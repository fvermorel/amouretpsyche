Chapter 1
The too beautiful princess
Three daughets
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The king and queen had three daughters. The first two were ravishing and graceful but the third was the most beautiful ever seen on Earth. People of all countries, smitten, only spoke of her. Her admirers would wait hours in the streets to be able to briefly see her when she did her walks. Women would imitate her hair dress, her make-up, her upkeep, not to try and imitate her as much as to give her homage. People adored her so that bit by bit, they began to forget the cult of the Goddess Aphrodite.
Aphrodite
 ⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aphrodite, the goddess of beauty, got wind of the reputation of Psyche and despised her. It was out of the question to have a simple mortal obscure her. She called her son, Cupid and demanded he avenge her. Psyche must be wed with the most horrible being out there so that her grace and beauty would permanently be tarnished in the hearts and memories of men. Eros armed himself with his bow and arrows and flew off right away.
Psyche is boring
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
For a long time, the two eldest daughters of the king were married to nearby lords, but Psyche was so beautiful that no one would dare take her hand: how could a simple mortal wed a woman like her? Therefore, the princess languished in the palace, sadly single and at the great sadness of her parents. Her father the king decided to consult the oracle of Delphi so that the gods could point him in a direction.
The oracle
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
But the response of the oracle would throw the entire kingdom in great worry: by the mouth of the oracle, the gods asked that Psyche was brought to a great rock outcrop that overlooked the sea. Then a horrible monster would come to her and instead of a wedding, she would be devoured. While he was hoping for a solution to wed his daughter, he instead got a death sentence! When they learned the terrible news, the king, the queen, their daughters and the entire kingdom spilled torrents of tears.
Ce fils de Zeus, qui délivrera Prométhée, c’est Héraklès, le plus grand des héros grec, celui qui accomplira les fameux douze travaux.
