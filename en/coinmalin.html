The divination

When the father of Psyche went to consult an oracle, he does something that is very common amongst the Greeks. Because the unknown worries them, men seek to discover what they don’t know. In nearly all societies, there are magic practices that are supposed to help men know their future.

Seers

Seers are people who can predict the future. Greek legend recounts a number that are famous, such as Tiresias or Calchas. Here, in the adventures of Asterix “the Seer” by Uderzo and Goscinny, a seer is shown – and in this case he’s actually a crook.

D.R.

Dreams

For the ancients, dreams are messages that gods send to humans. Here you see a painting by Peter Von Cornelius representing the prophet Joseph who interprets the dream of the pharaoh.

D.R.

Sacrifices

Sometimes to know the future, it gets more complicated: one has to kill an animal and read the future in its entrails. Notably, it is the specialty of the Haruspices in Rome. Here you see a bas relief showing the sacrifice of a bull.

D.R.

Astrology

Astrology, not to be confused by Astronomy which is a science, consists in reading the future in the stars. The signs of the Zodiac, which you have probably heard referenced, come from this field of study.

D.R.

I-Ching

The I-Ching is a Chinese Divination technique. It is a manual written in the 8th century before Christ and it allows people to use a complicated set of symbols and signs.

D.R.

The tarot

One of the most recent and popular ways to predict the future is the reading of Tarot cards. These are then analyzed and interpreted by a Fortune Teller. Example: the tarot of Marseille

D.R.

The Wind

For the Ancient Greeks, the wind was an autonomous force, a god who would blow where he wants. We now know that even if he follows very complicated rules, the wind is a natural phenomenon that can be scientifically explained.

Atmosphere

There is wind on this earth because our planet is covered by an atmosphere. The atmosphere resembles gases that separate land and sea with space. Without it we would not be able to breathe and nothing could live on earth.

Pressure and Heat

On earth there are places that are warmer than others. When the wind is warm, it dilates and takes more volume. This creates a draw that moves the air. The air can also cool and contract itself, also causing a movement. Wind is exactly that: the movement of air.

D.R.

The surface of the earth

The poles are the coldest places on Earth and as one gets close to the equator the warmer it gets. But certain elements can affect wind: clouds, mountains and seas which have a different temperature than continents, etc.

D.R.

Storms and tornadoes

A tornado is a whirlwind of strong winds. This can happen when opposing wings run into each other: they can then wrap themselves around each other and cause great destruction.

Air Drafts and Experiment

But not all winds are as impressive and as dangerous. If it is cold outside and your room is very warm, you can try this experiment: open a window and the warm air will push out to the outside. That too, is wind.

Utilizing wind

The wind is a resource that renews itself, unlike petroleum, coal or uranium. In antiquity, people already used sailboats. Later on people built air powered wind mills to grind grains. Today’s windmills produce electricity.

Cupid / Eros

Eros is the god of love. Sometimes he has the appearance of a little child, sometimes a young man.

D.R.

Aphrodite

Aphrodite is the goddess of love and beauty. She was born when Chronos cut a bit of flesh from Ouranos (Uranus). Her story is told in the book “the origin of the world”

D.R.

Aeolus

Aeolus is the god of the wind. Some say he is the son of Poseidon, the god of the seas.

D.R.

Hermes

Hermes is the god of merchants and thieves. He is the son of Zeus and a goddess called Maia.

D.R.

Hera

Hera is a goddess. She is the wife of Zeus, the king of gods.

D.R.

Persephone

Persephone is the wife of Hades and the daughter of Demeter. She spends half of her time with her husband in hell and the other half with her mother on earth. It is because of her that summer follows into winter as is told in our book “the origin of the world”

D.R.

Psyche

Psyche is our hero. Her name means spirit.
